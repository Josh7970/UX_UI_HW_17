My passion for creating intuitive and user-centered designs began while studying Information systems in college. It was then that I discovered the similarities between design and analytics, and the importance of organization and visual appeal in creating a positive user experience. However, it was while traveling across Southeast Asia that I truly understood the correlation between design and the emotions it evokes. I saw firsthand how a person's experience can be greatly impacted by the interactions they have with their environment. This realization only solidified my belief that a good design should be meaningful, purposeful, and memorable to truly benefit users. Without this understanding, design is nothing more than a problem-creating tool. My goal as a designer is to create experiences that not only look good, but also solve real problems for users
